About

I'm curretnly a master's student in robotics at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Prof. Fernando De la Torre in the Human Sensing Laboratory. I obtained my master degree at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen, advised by Dr. Li Liu. Previously, I was a research intern at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA), working with Dr. Jindong Wang and Dr. Xing Xie. I am extremely fortunate to have been advised by my exceptional advisors throughout my journey.

I work at the intersection of trustworthy AI and LLMs, bringing principles from fairness, privacy, and robustness into how I train and evaluate large language models so they behave reasonably and reliably in open‑world settings. My background spans diagnosing dataset and representation issues, strengthening models against adversarial and distribution shifts, formal guarantees of AI models under differential privacy and post-training techniques for LLMs.

Hardware Projects

My friends (Ziyin Lu, Sihan Chen, Yuxuan Li and Mianhuang Hunag) and I runs Elecholic, a startup teams focuses on robotic hardware since 2016. We provide standardized motor controller system and efficient AGV (automated guided vehicle) solutions. Below are two selected projects:

MecaRun Series
A project of professional robot chassis control system.
Enhanced VESC
An enhanced version of the open-source project VESC.
EXCAP — Optical–Inertial Full-Body Tracking for VR
A full-body motion tracking system that fuses optical and inertial sensing for room-scale VR. I co-designed the sensor fusion and automatic calibration pipeline. The system was validated through a successful Kickstarter campaign and user testing.
