About

I'm a research associate in the human sensing lab at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), advised by Prof. Torre. I obtained my master degree at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), Shenzhen, advised by Dr. Li Liu. Previously, I was a research intern at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA), working with Dr. Jindong Wang and Dr. Xing Xie.

My vision is twofold: 1) to construct private, fair, robust and explainable models towards more trustworthy AI, making them reliable for the open world; and 2) to boost the performance of neural networks in low/imperfect-resource scenarios, thereby broadening their application. Hence, I pay extensive attention to the progress of robustness, fairness, explainability, differential privacy and learning from imperfect data.
